Schematic

  • PT100 is an RTD sensor, it's resistance changes with temperature.
  • It is 100 Ohms at zero degree celcius.
  • The resistance is measured by connecting PT100 to a Constant Current Source. The voltage drop is proportional to the resistance.
  • It is measured after some amplification, since the voltage is less than200 mV even at 100 degC.

Instructions

  • The PT100 sensor is connected from the 1.1mA constant current source to ground.
  • The voltage across PT100 is monitored using A3.
  • The 1K resistor from Rg to Ground, amplifies the signal connected to A3 by 11 times
  • The temperature and time ranges can be selected before starting to record the data.
